Non-Invasive Glucometer

Problem

Checking the glucose level in blood in the traditional way is requires blood sample which in turn need piercing the finger for blood. Also, the devices used are expensive and requires replacement of test strips each time.


User

People who require frequent monitoring of their blood glucose level for the proper intake of insulin.


My Role

The solution concept was a device which uses photometry to measure the blood glucose level by the capacity of blood to allow the light through the finger. So my role was to design a casing which concisely holds a 2 light array and 2 light sensors interfaced with a microcontroller and a display.


Design Process

Many designs were discussed with the team to get finalized with a design. The design was inspired by a fracture finger support provided by the physicians. The design was modified to contain the microcontroller and the photometry setup. Along with that, the UX was maintained by its aesthetics and placing an HMI display to show the user with the readings.
